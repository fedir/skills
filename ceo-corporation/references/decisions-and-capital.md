# Decisions, capital, and people

A CEO's leverage comes from a few high-quality decisions and where they point scarce resources.
Optimize decision *quality* and *speed*, not the illusion of certainty.

## Deciding under uncertainty

- **Reversible vs irreversible.** Reversible (two-way-door) decisions: decide fast, delegate, learn
  by doing. Irreversible (one-way-door) decisions — a pivot, a big raise, a key hire/fire, a market
  commitment — get real deliberation. Most decisions are reversible and are being over-thought.
- **Think in bets and expected value**, not certainties. Weigh the upside, the probability, and —
  first — the **downside**. Avoid bets that can kill the company, however attractive the upside.
- **Opportunity cost is the true cost.** The question isn't "is this good?" but "is this the best use
  of this dollar / hire / month versus the alternatives?"
- **Decide, then commit.** Ambivalent half-commitment is worse than either choice. State the
  assumptions that would make you reverse, and set a checkpoint to test them.
- **Speed matters.** A good decision now usually beats a perfect one late. Set a decision deadline.

## Capital allocation

Allocating capital is one of the CEO's highest-leverage jobs.

- **Runway is oxygen.** Always know cash on hand, burn rate, and months of runway. Don't let it get
  short by surprise; raise or cut *before* you're forced to.
- **Fund the vital few; starve the rest.** Concentrate spend on the differentiating bets.
- **Watch unit economics** (contribution margin, CAC, LTV, payback period). Growth on broken unit
  economics accelerates death.
- **Invest vs return**: spend where the compounding return (moat, growth, retention) is highest;
  cut spend that doesn't earn its keep.
- **Fundraising** is a means, not a milestone: raise for a clear use that increases enterprise value,
  from investors who add more than money, at terms that keep the team motivated.

## People and organization

- **Hiring is the highest-leverage act.** A players raise the bar and hire A players; B players hire
  C players. Hire slowly for fit and strength; be honest that the wrong senior hire is very costly.
- **Manage out quickly and humanely.** The kindest thing is a fast, respectful exit; keeping a
  mis-hire hurts them, the team, and the mission. Firing well is a CEO skill.
- **Set direction and standards, then delegate.** Own the *what* and the *why*; let strong people
  own the *how*. Micromanagement caps the company at your bandwidth.
- **Org design follows strategy.** Structure teams around the outcomes that matter; align incentives
  to the goals (people do what they're measured and paid for). Keep it as flat and simple as the
  work allows.
- **Culture is what you tolerate and reward**, not what's on a poster. Model it; hire and fire for it.

## Prioritization tools

- **Impact vs effort / opportunity cost** — do the high-impact, load-bearing work first.
- **One metric that matters** for the phase — rally the org around the current constraint.
- **Kill list** — explicitly stop low-value work to free resources for the vital few.
