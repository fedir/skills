# Strategy and focus

Strategy is a small set of choices about where to play and how to win — and, just as importantly,
what not to do. It is not a list of everything you'd like to happen.

## Vision, mission, strategy — don't confuse them

- **Mission** — why the company exists; the enduring purpose.
- **Vision** — the future you're trying to create; where you're going.
- **Strategy** — the *how*: the specific choices (market, customer, positioning, capabilities) that
  give you an unfair advantage in getting there.
- **Plan/OKRs** — the near-term execution against the strategy.

A real strategy makes hard choices explicit. If your "strategy" offends no alternative and rules
nothing out, it's a wish, not a strategy.

## Focus: the vital few

- **Pick the 1–3 things that matter most this year** and pour resources there. Most initiatives are
  a distraction dressed as opportunity.
- **Saying no is the job.** Every yes to a good idea is a no to a great one; every unfocused bet
  starves the focused ones. Maintain a visible "not now / not us" list.
- **Sequence, don't parallelize everything.** Win one beachhead before opening a second front.
- Beware **shiny-object chasing** and competitor-reactive strategy. Run your own game.

## Where to play / how to win

- **Customer & market**: who exactly is the customer, what job are you doing for them, and is the
  market big enough (and reachable) to matter?
- **Positioning**: why you, not the alternatives (including "do nothing")? What's the sharp wedge?
- **Moat / durable advantage**: network effects, switching costs, economies of scale, brand,
  proprietary tech/data, or a structural cost edge. A business with no moat competes on price
  forever. Name yours and how it compounds.
- **Focus resources on the differentiator**; outsource/buy the undifferentiated.

## Turning strategy into goals

- **OKRs / clear goals**: a few measurable objectives that ladder up to the strategy. Outcome
  metrics (revenue, retention, activation), not activity metrics.
- **Cascade** so every team can see how their work serves the vital few.
- **Review on a cadence** and be willing to change course when the evidence says so — persistence on
  the mission, flexibility on the path.

## Signals your strategy is weak

- It's a list of goals, not choices; nothing is ruled out.
- You can't state your durable advantage in a sentence.
- Resources are spread evenly across many bets ("peanut butter").
- It changes with every competitor move or board meeting.
