# Communication and stakeholders

A CEO's leverage runs through other people, so communication *is* the job — repeating the strategy,
aligning stakeholders, and telling the truth, especially when it's hard. Tailor the message to each
audience's concerns; keep the underlying facts consistent.

## Principles

- **Repeat the strategy relentlessly.** People need to hear direction many times before it sticks.
  When you're tired of saying it, they're starting to hear it.
- **Candor builds trust.** Share the bad news early and straight. Trust is a reserve you build in
  good times and spend in crises; sugar-coating burns it.
- **Same facts, different framing.** Never tell different stakeholders contradictory stories —
  emphasize what each cares about, but keep the truth consistent.
- **Clarity over polish.** Say the point first, plainly. (See the `tech-writer` skill for structure.)

## Board

- **No surprises.** The board should hear big problems from you first, not discover them.
- **Come with a point of view**, not just a data dump: here's the situation, here's my
  recommendation, here's where I want your input. Use the board for the few decisions and
  connections where they add most.
- **Standard update**: metrics vs plan, wins, the top 2–3 risks/asks, cash/runway, and key
  decisions. Honest on misses, with the plan to fix them.

## Investors

- **Sell the vision, prove the traction.** Pair an ambitious, credible story with real numbers and a
  clear use of funds.
- **Keep warm between raises** with concise, regular updates (metrics, highs, lows, asks) — investors
  fund lines they've watched execute. The lows build more credibility than the highs.

## Employees / all-hands

- **Context creates ownership.** Explain the *why* behind decisions so people can make good calls
  without you. Connect their work to the mission and the current one-metric-that-matters.
- **Address the elephant.** In hard times (layoffs, misses, pivots) name it directly, own it, and
  give a clear path forward. Silence breeds worse rumors.
- **Recognize and reinforce** the behaviors and wins that embody the strategy and culture.

## Customers

- Represent the customer's voice internally; be visible to key accounts. In incidents, communicate
  early, honestly, and with the fix and timeline — over-communicate rather than go dark.

## Crisis communication

When something goes badly wrong (outage, breach, PR issue, layoffs):

1. **Get the facts** fast; don't speculate publicly.
2. **Own it early and honestly** — acknowledge, take responsibility, don't spin.
3. **Say what you're doing about it** and by when; then do it.
4. **Communicate proactively** to each stakeholder in the right order (often: those affected, team,
   board/investors, public) with a consistent message.
5. **Follow up** with the resolution and what changes so it doesn't recur.

The instinct to hide makes it worse. Speed, ownership, and honesty contain the damage.
